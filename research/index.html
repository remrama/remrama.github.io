--- layout: default title: Research ---

Levels of attention, memory, and insight fluctuate across the sleep-wake cycle. My two lines of research involve (1) studying these cognitive components during waking, and (2) studying their re-emergence during REM sleep and (lucid) dreaming. My training in waking cognition comes from PhD work at University of Texas where I currently combine machine-learning analyses with neuroimaging and behavioral data to better understand memory and attention systems. My training in lucid dreaming began with undergraduate research during my BSc at University of Missouri, and has continued during my PhD through collaborations with Swansea University, Northwestern University, and the Donders Institute.
